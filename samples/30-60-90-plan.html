30-60-90 Day Plan

For ESG Reporting / Sustainability Project Coordinator roles · Lund, Sweden

The goal of my first 90 days would be to turn scattered ESG information into a structured, trackable, evidence-ready workflow that the team can defend in front of auditors, customers, and investors.

Operating principles

  • Listen before changing — map what already works before redesigning anything.
  • Evidence-first — every claim should point to an owner, a source, and a date.
  • Project-style cadence — weekly status, monthly review, quarterly readiness check.
  • No exaggeration — favour CSRD/ESRS readiness language over compliance claims.

The plan

TimelineFocusOutput
First 30 daysUnderstand company ESG data, reporting scope, and stakeholders. Shadow weekly meetings, interview data owners, and audit existing disclosures.Disclosure inventory + stakeholder map + initial gap-impressions note.
First 60 daysBuild gap map, evidence register, and data-owner tracker. Identify weak claims and missing KPIs. Run one supplier ESG dry-run.ESRS / VSME mapping + evidence tracker + supplier ESG questionnaire draft.
First 90 daysSupport reporting workflow and readiness review. Set up the cadence for the next reporting cycle and an audit-readiness checklist.ESG reporting dashboard + 6-month action plan + audit-readiness checklist.

Risks I’d watch in the first 90 days

  • Data owner ambiguity — two people own the same KPI and neither feels accountable.
  • Single-source dependency — one spreadsheet that nobody else can recover if it’s lost.
  • Late evidence — claims agreed on in marketing without evidence in finance / ops.
  • Supplier non-response — high-risk suppliers ignoring the questionnaire with no escalation path.

This is a sample plan, structured around how I’d join an ESG / sustainability team. The exact priorities would be tuned to the company’s reporting framework (CSRD, VSME, voluntary), assurance scope, and supplier landscape on day one.
